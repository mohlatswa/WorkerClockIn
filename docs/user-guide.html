WorkClock — Worker Guide

Everything you need to know about clocking in, clocking out, and using the WorkClock app on your phone.

What is WorkClock?

WorkClock is your company's digital attendance system. Instead of signing a paper register or using a card reader, you clock in and out using your smartphone — right from your browser, no app download required (though you can install it if you want, see Install as an App).

Every time you clock in, the app records the time, your GPS location, and how you identified yourself (PIN, fingerprint, or face). Your manager can then see your attendance records from the admin panel.

WorkClock only lets you clock in when you are at the correct location — so you need to be physically at your workplace.

Accessing the App

Your employer will give you a link that looks like this:

https://mohlatswa.github.io/WorkerClockIn/?c=COMPANYCODE

Open this link in your phone's web browser (Chrome or Safari work best). Bookmark it or save it to your home screen so you can find it quickly every day.

💡

Important: Always use the link your employer gave you — it contains your company's code. Without the company code, the app may not recognise your Employee ID.

Your First Sign-In

The first time you sign in, your phone is registered to your account. After that, you can only sign in from this same phone (unless your manager resets your account).

Open your company's WorkClock link in your browser.

Tap the large Employee ID field in the middle of the screen and type your Employee ID (e.g. EMP001). This is case-insensitive.

Tap Continue. The app will find your account and show your name.

Enter your PIN on the keypad. Tap the digits — you don't need to press a submit button, the PIN is checked automatically after 4 digits.

If your PIN is correct, you will be taken to your worker dashboard. Your phone is now registered to your account.

📱

Your PIN was set by your manager when your account was created. If you do not know your PIN, ask your manager to tell you or reset it.

How to Clock In

After signing in, you will see your worker dashboard. The main feature is the large round button in the centre of the screen.

Worker Dashboard
▶
Clock In

✅ Within 45m of Head Office

Sign in using your Employee ID and PIN (or fingerprint / face — see below).

The app will ask for your location. Tap Allow when your browser asks for location permission. This is required for the Clock In button to activate.

Wait a moment while the app finds your GPS position. You will see a message like "Within 45m of Head Office" when you are close enough.

Tap the large green Clock In button. A success screen will appear briefly showing your name and the time.

You are now clocked in. The button changes to red Clock Out. Today's record appears above showing your clock-in time.

📍

If the button shows "Too far from workplace", you are outside the allowed area. You must be physically at your registered workplace to clock in. Moving closer to the building usually fixes this within a few seconds.

How to Clock Out

At the end of your shift, clock out so your hours are recorded correctly. Forgetting to clock out means your attendance will show "Still In" — let your manager know if this happens.

Open the WorkClock app on your phone. If you are still signed in, your dashboard will appear. If not, sign in again.

You will see the large red Clock Out button (it shows this because you are currently clocked in).

Make sure your location is still showing — you need to be at the workplace to clock out too.

Tap Clock Out. A confirmation screen will appear with your name and clock-out time.

Today's record updates to show both your clock-in and clock-out time, and the hours worked.

Viewing Your Attendance History

Your worker dashboard shows your attendance history for the past 14 days in a list below the clock-in button. Each row shows:

  • The day and date (e.g. Mon, 12 May)
  • Your clock-in and clock-out times (e.g. 08:15 → 17:02)
  • Total hours worked for that day
  • The method you used to sign in (PIN, Biometric, or Face)
  • Whether the record is Done (you clocked out) or Active (still clocked in)

If you need records older than 14 days, ask your manager to check the admin attendance report.

Signing Out of the App

Signing out of the app is different from clocking out — clocking out records the end of your shift, while signing out logs you out of the app so others cannot use your account on a shared device.

To sign out, scroll to the bottom of your worker dashboard and tap the Sign Out button. You will be returned to the home screen. Your shift record is not affected.

On most personal phones, you do not need to sign out every time — the app remembers you. Only sign out if you share your device with other people.

Changing Your PIN

You can change your own PIN at any time from your worker dashboard — you do not need to ask your manager.

Sign in and open your worker dashboard.

Scroll down to find the Change PIN card and tap Change PIN.

Enter your current PIN, then enter your new PIN twice (the two entries must match).

Tap Save New PIN. You will see a confirmation message. Use the new PIN the next time you sign in.

🔑

Choose a PIN that is easy for you to remember but not obvious to others. Avoid PINs like 1234 or your birth year. Your PIN must be 4–10 digits.

Your Three Sign-In Methods

🔢

PIN

Enter your Employee ID, then tap your 4–6 digit PIN on the keypad. The most reliable method — works on every device and browser.

📷

Face Recognition

The app uses your phone's camera to recognise your face. Tap Use Face Recognition on the home screen. Your face must be enrolled by your manager first.

👤

Fingerprint / Face ID

Use your phone's built-in fingerprint reader or Face ID. Tap Use Fingerprint / Face ID on the auth screen. Your biometric must be registered first.

ℹ

All three methods are equally valid. Your attendance record will show which method you used. If one method is not working, try another.

Setting Up Face Recognition

Face recognition is set up by your manager — you cannot do this yourself. Ask your manager to enrol your face from the admin panel.

However, if your manager has already enrolled your face, you will see a Face Enrolment card on your worker dashboard if your face has not yet been saved. You can tap it to capture your own face.

Tips for a good face scan

  • Face the camera directly, looking straight ahead
  • Make sure you are in a well-lit area — avoid dark rooms or strong backlighting (e.g. sitting with a bright window behind you)
  • Remove sunglasses or large hats if possible
  • Keep a natural expression — you do not need to smile

If face recognition keeps failing (saying "Face not recognised"), ask your manager to re-enrol your face, ideally in the same lighting conditions you normally work in.

Setting Up Fingerprint / Face ID

Biometric registration can be done by your manager (from the admin panel) or by yourself from your worker dashboard.

On your worker dashboard, if you have not yet registered a biometric, you will see a Fingerprint Registration card. Tap Register Fingerprint / Face ID. Your phone will ask you to use your fingerprint or Face ID to confirm. This links your phone's biometric to your WorkClock account.

Important notes

  • Biometric sign-in only works on the same phone and browser where it was registered. If you get a new phone, your manager must reset your security and re-register your biometric.
  • Your fingerprint data is stored only on your device by your phone's operating system — WorkClock never receives or stores your actual fingerprint.
  • If your phone is replaced or your browser data is cleared, you will need to register again.

Installing WorkClock on Your Phone

You can add WorkClock to your phone's home screen so it opens like a regular app — no browser bar, no searching for the link.

On Android (Chrome)

Open your company's WorkClock link in Chrome.

Tap the three-dot menu button (⋮) in the top-right corner of Chrome.

Tap Add to Home screen.

Tap Add on the confirmation dialog. The WorkClock icon will appear on your home screen.

On iPhone (Safari)

Open your company's WorkClock link in Safari (not Chrome — Safari is required on iPhone for home screen installation).

Tap the Share button at the bottom of the screen (the square with an arrow pointing up).

Scroll down in the share menu and tap Add to Home Screen.

Tap Add in the top-right corner. The WorkClock icon will appear on your home screen.

💡

Once installed, the app opens in full-screen mode without any browser address bar — it feels just like a regular app. Your company's portal link and your sign-in state are remembered automatically.

What to Do if You Are Locked Out

There are two types of lock-out. Here is what to do for each:

Too many wrong PINs (temporary lock)

If you enter the wrong PIN 5 times in a row, your account is locked for 30 minutes. You will see a message like: "Account locked. Try again in 28 minutes."

You have two options:

  • Wait 30 minutes. The lock clears automatically. You do not need to contact anyone.
  • Contact your manager. They can unlock your account immediately using the Reset Security option in the admin panel.

Wrong device (device binding)

If you see the message "This account is bound to another device", it means your account was registered on a different phone. This happens if:

  • You switched to a new phone
  • You cleared your browser's data or cookies
  • Someone else accidentally signed in with your Employee ID on their device

Contact your manager. Only a manager can reset device binding. After the reset, sign in again from your current phone — it will become the new registered device.

Forgot your PIN

If you cannot remember your PIN, you can set a new one yourself from your worker dashboard using the Change PIN card — but you will need to enter your current PIN to do so. If you have forgotten your current PIN completely, contact your manager and ask them to reset it. They can set a new PIN by editing your worker profile in the admin panel.

📞

Always keep your manager's contact number saved so you can reach them quickly if you have a sign-in issue before your shift.

Privacy Notice

WorkClock collects the following information about you when you use the app:

DataWhat it isWhy it is collected
GPS Location Your phone's GPS coordinates at the time of clock-in and clock-out To confirm you are physically at your registered workplace when clocking in. Stored as part of the attendance record.
Clock-in / Clock-out times The exact date and time you clock in and out To record your attendance hours for your employer
Authentication method Whether you used PIN, fingerprint, or face recognition For attendance record completeness
Device ID A randomly generated identifier stored in your phone's browser storage Device binding — to ensure only your registered device can sign in to your account. This is not linked to your phone's hardware serial number.
Face descriptor A set of 128 numbers that mathematically represents your face. Not a photo. Face recognition sign-in. Stored in the database. Only used to identify you when you choose to use face recognition.
Biometric credential ID A reference identifier for your fingerprint or Face ID credential. Not your actual fingerprint. Fingerprint / Face ID sign-in. Your actual biometric data stays on your device — WorkClock only stores a reference ID.

What WorkClock does NOT do

  • It does not take photos of you or record video
  • It does not store your actual fingerprint — only a reference number managed by your phone's operating system
  • It does not track your location continuously — only at the moment you tap Clock In or Clock Out
  • It does not share your data with any third party outside your company

Your attendance data is stored in a secure cloud database managed by your employer. If you have questions about how your data is used, please speak to your manager.

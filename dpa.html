Data Processing Agreement

Under the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA) · WorkClock-In attendance system

1. Parties

This Agreement is between:

  • [CLIENT COMPANY NAME] ("the Responsible Party"), and
  • Reatlegile N Solutions (Pty) Ltd (Reg. No. 2026/139315/07), trading as Reatlegile Solutions ("the Operator"), provider of WorkClock-In.

2. Subject matter

The Operator processes personal information on behalf of the Responsible Party solely to provide the WorkClock-In staff time-and-attendance service.

3. Nature & purpose of processing

  • Categories of data subjects: the Responsible Party's employees/workers.
  • Categories of personal information: name, employee ID, job title, contact details, hashed PIN, clock-in/out times, GPS location, sign-in device, and (where the data subject consents) biometric templates (facial recognition / device security key).
  • Purpose: recording attendance, hours, lateness and overtime; identity verification at clock-in.

4. Operator obligations

The Operator shall:

  • Process personal information only on the documented instructions of the Responsible Party and only for the purpose above;
  • Treat all personal information as confidential and ensure persons authorised to process it are bound by confidentiality;
  • Implement appropriate, reasonable technical and organisational security measures (s19 POPIA) — see §6;
  • Not engage another sub-processor without the Responsible Party's prior general authorisation (see §5);
  • Assist the Responsible Party in responding to data-subject requests and in meeting its POPIA obligations;
  • Notify the Responsible Party without undue delay on becoming aware of any security compromise affecting their data;
  • On termination, delete or return all personal information at the Responsible Party's choice.

5. Sub-processors

The Responsible Party authorises the use of Supabase (cloud database hosting, EU region) as a sub-processor. The Operator remains liable for the sub-processor's compliance and shall inform the Responsible Party of any intended change.

6. Security measures

  • Credentials (PINs/passwords) stored as one-way hashes;
  • Encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS);
  • Row-level security and server-side access controls; the public application key cannot directly write attendance, worker or workplace records;
  • Administrator session tokens and account-lockout protection;
  • Biometric processing only with the data subject's explicit consent, with withdrawal/deletion on request.

7. Cross-border transfer

Personal information may be processed on servers in the European Union, which affords an adequate level of protection under POPIA s72.

8. Data-subject rights & breach

The Operator shall assist the Responsible Party in giving effect to data subjects' rights of access, correction, deletion and objection, and in reporting any security compromise to the Information Regulator and affected data subjects as required by s22 POPIA.

9. Duration & termination

This Agreement runs for the term of the WorkClock-In service agreement. On termination the Operator will delete or return all personal information within [30] days, save where retention is required by law.

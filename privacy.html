WorkClock — Privacy Notice

How we handle your personal information · Compliant with POPIA (Act 4 of 2013)

Last updated: 18 June 2026  ·  Effective: 18 June 2026

WorkClock is a staff time-and-attendance system. This notice explains what personal information we collect when you use it, why, how we protect it, and the rights you have under the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA).

1. Who is responsible for your information

3. Biometric data (special personal information)

If you choose to use face or fingerprint clock-in, we store a mathematical template of your face (not a photograph) and/or a device-bound security key. We never store an actual fingerprint image.

This is voluntary and consent-based. Biometric clock-in is optional — you can always use a PIN instead. We only enrol your biometrics after you tick the consent box, and you may withdraw consent at any time by asking your administrator, who will delete the biometric data.

4. Why we may process your information (lawful basis)

  • Performance of your employment — recording attendance and hours.
  • Consent — for optional biometric clock-in.
  • Legitimate interests of the employer — preventing time fraud (e.g. buddy-clocking).

5. How long we keep it

Attendance records are kept while you are an active employee and for any period required by law or your employer's payroll/record-keeping obligations. Biometric data is deleted when you withdraw consent or leave employment.

6. How we protect it

  • PINs and passwords are hashed (never stored in plain text).
  • All data is transmitted over encrypted (HTTPS) connections.
  • Database access is restricted by row-level security and server-side access controls; the app cannot be used to alter attendance directly.
  • Administrator accounts use session tokens and account lockout protection.

7. Who we share it with

Your information is accessible to your employer's authorised administrators and to Reatlegile Solutions for support and operation. It is stored with Supabase under a data-processing agreement. We do not sell your information or use it for advertising. Some processing occurs on servers in the European Union, which provides an adequate level of data protection.

8. Your rights under POPIA

  • Access the personal information we hold about you.
  • Ask us to correct or delete information that is inaccurate or no longer needed.
  • Object to processing, or withdraw biometric consent, at any time.
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator.

To exercise these rights, contact your administrator or Reatlegile Solutions (details below).
